Gesture Craft

Bridging Play and Learning ‍Immerse yourself in a groundbreaking world of education. This visionary project takes the beloved Minecraft and infuses it with the magic of gestures, offering children an unparalleled hands-on experience. "Gesture - Craft" empowers kids to navigate and build within the virtual realm using their own hands.

Objective

This project aims to investigate how interactive gestures and control gestures, as identified through gesture coding, impact children's cognitive processes and learning outcomes in educational settings. The goal is to create an inclusive learning environment, especially beneficial for children with disabilities, by combining accessible design with embodied cognition to promote novel forms of engagement and spatial reasoning.

Methods

In the first user study, 15 participants aged 6-17 from a STEAM summer camp were surveyed for gaming gestures preferences. Seven participants were introduced to a gesture-based gameplay system without specific gesture details, while eight were given all details. Post-play surveys were used to gather feedback, influencing adjustments to the gesture recognition program. A second round with ten 9-12 year olds involved Minecraft-related spatial reasoning tasks, with data collected on gestures and participant experiences for further refinement.

Findings

While hand poses may lead to fatigue during movement and navigation tasks, users report easier viewfinder and object manipulation. Increased usage of embodied gestures in spatial cognition tasks is noted among players who extensively utilized the gestural interface, and youth suggestions for complex in-game tasks expand the design space for embodied interactions. The study aims to develop and assess mechanistic embodiments, such as loops and modularization, for their potential roles in spatial reasoning and computing education, highlighting connections between gesture use patterns and competencies in spatial reasoning through gesture data analysis.
